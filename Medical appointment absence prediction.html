Introduction¶

In this project we will investigate together data related to medical appointments in Brazil, this dataset collects information from 100k medical appointments and focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment.

Content¶

The project data consists of 14 associated variables, the most important one 'show up' variable as this is our main question topic.

scholarship variable means this concept Bolsa Famlia is a social welfare program of the Government of Brazil, part of the Fome Zero network of federal assistance programs.

Data Cleaning¶

There is no missing values in any columns

As we can see the data types needs to be fixed for several columns: PatientId and AppointmentID are currently numeric where in fact they have should been string or object as they have no numeric meaning. ScheduledDay and AppointmentDay should be converted from object to date-time Other remarks: No-show should be recoded and converted into integer

Conclusions¶

Impact of variables to attendence ratio:

According to our dataset we found that :

* there is an equal attendance ratio between male and female
* persons with no scholarship have higher attendance ratio
* Higher attendance ratio for hypertension persons
* Higher attendance ratio for Diabbetes persons
* Almost equal attendance ratio for Alcoholism persons
* Lower attendance level for persons who received SMS.
* We can also notice as the degree of disability increases the probability of presence decreases

Impact of Age :

* The mean age of all patients is 37
* The patients in the youth group (15~24 years old) showed highest absence ratio, while those in the seniors group (65           years old and over) showed lowest absence ratio. 
* This means people with older age is highly stick with medical appointment

Impact of neighourhood:

* The dataset contains 81 neighborhood with variance samples 
* All neighborhood have the same absence ratio
* This variance in samples affects two neighborhood negatively what leads to excluding them

Limitations :

* The dataset collects information for 100k medical appointments in Brazil, according to the last statistic in 2019               Brazil's population is 211 million, which means that we won't be able to provide accurate analytical information.
* There is a variance in the gathered data samples across the neighborhood, we found that there is a neighborhood that           contains more than 7000 samples and the others only have one sample.
* while wrangling our dataset found that there was an age less than zero which doesn't make sense, which leads us to             drop such kind of dataset
